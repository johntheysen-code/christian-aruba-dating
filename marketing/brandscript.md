# Amor y Fe — BrandScript

Donald Miller's StoryBrand framework applied to Amor y Fe. This is the messaging cheat sheet behind every headline, email, ad, and pastor pitch.

When you're stuck on copy, ask: *"Which part of the BrandScript am I expressing? Is it landing clearly?"*

---

## 1. CHARACTER — *Who is the hero?*

A Christian single adult on Aruba — at **any life stage** — who wants to be married to a believer who shares their faith and way of life.

This includes:

- Young professionals in their 20s and 30s
- Singles in their 30s and 40s who feel local dating options are exhausted
- **Believers 50+ entering a new chapter** — divorced, widowed, or never married — looking for a faithful second-half partner
- Newcomers to the island who need to find community

Faith and seriousness about marriage are the qualifiers — not age.

---

## 2. PROBLEM — *What stands in the hero's way?*

### External (the tangible obstacle)
> *"I can't find faithful Christian singles to date here. The pool feels exhausted, and the apps that exist don't filter on what actually matters to me. The 50+ apps treat me like a senior demographic, not a person of faith."*

### Internal (how it makes them feel)
> *"I feel lonely. Sometimes hopeless. I wonder if I'm being too picky. I wonder if I should just compromise on faith. I wonder if it's even possible at my age."*

### Philosophical (why it's wrong)
> *"It's not right that serious believers don't have a tool built for them — at every stage of life. The world's dating apps serve everyone but us, and the senior apps don't take faith seriously. God didn't write us out of the marriage story because we got older or because life took a hard turn."*

---

## 3. GUIDE — *Why trust Amor y Fe?*

### Empathy
> *"We get it. Built by an Aruban Christian who lived this. We know the church community. We know what it's like to feel out of options at 25 — and we know what it's like to start over at 55. Both stories matter here."*

### Authority
- 22-question compatibility quiz covering what *actually* matters in Christian marriage — Scripture, prayer life, marriage timing, children, carnival, Sabbath, cohabitation, media, lifestyle
- Restricted to one-man-one-woman matching, aligned with the biblical view
- Verified-real members through Facebook sign-in (no anonymous catfishing)
- Built locally, in Papiamento-friendly language, with knowledge of every Aruban town and church
- Welcoming to widowed, divorced, and never-married believers at every stage

---

## 4. PLAN — *Clear steps to take*

### Process plan (what they do)
1. **Sign in with Facebook** *(verified, real, 30 seconds)*
2. **Complete your profile and take the quiz** *(~15 minutes — yes, really)*
3. **Browse believers who fit** *(compatibility scores tell you who actually aligns)*
4. **Message safely after a mutual match** *(no cold outreach, no spam)*

### Agreement plan (what we promise)
- Free to use
- Your profile is only visible to other signed-in members
- Personal details are gradually revealed after mutual interest, never before
- Block and report tools are one tap
- You can delete your account permanently at any time, no questions

---

## 5. CALL TO ACTION

### Direct CTA (what we want them to do)
> **"Take the quiz"** → `/quiz`

Or, for first-time visitors:
> **"Continue with Facebook"** → sign in

### Transitional CTA (low-commitment first step)
> **"How it works"** — anchor link to the homepage steps
> **"Common questions"** — FAQ section

---

## 6. FAILURE TO AVOID — *What's at stake if they do nothing?*

- Another year of swipe culture and disappointing dates
- Settling for someone who doesn't share your faith
- A lifetime of "what could have been" because you didn't bother to look
- For older singles: writing yourself out of the marriage story prematurely
- Drift from community, isolation, slow erosion of hope

---

## 7. SUCCESS PICTURE — *What does life look like after?*

- Walking into church Sunday with your spouse
- Building a home rooted in shared faith and shared convictions
- For young couples: raising children in a marriage that honors God
- For seasoned believers: a faithful companion in the second half — *"two are better than one, because they have a good reward for their toil"* (Ecclesiastes 4:9)
- The deep peace of finally being equally yoked
- A marriage you can tell your church family about with joy

## 🎯 Using the BrandScript in your work

| Homepage headline | Character + Success |
| Sub-headline / lede | Problem (external + internal) |

| Pastor pitch | Guide + Plan + how we protect their congregants |
| Onboarding email | Plan + Direct CTA + Success |

## One-paragraph "controlling idea" *(memorize this)*

> Christian singles on Aruba — at every age and life stage — face a problem the world ignores: there's no dating tool built for serious believers who share both their faith and their way of life. Amor y Fe is the local guide that solves this with a deep compatibility quiz, verified members, and a marriage-first community — so believers can stop swiping, stop settling, and start building the kind of marriage that honors God.

That paragraph is the spine. Everything you write should be a flavor of it.
